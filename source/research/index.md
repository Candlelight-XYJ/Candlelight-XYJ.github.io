## Research Summary

## AI for Perturbation Modeling
<img src="/researchExperience/ReseachSummary.png" alt="Research summary" style="max-width: 100%; height: auto;">

My recent research focuses on AI methods for cellular perturbation modeling, with an emphasis on reliability, interpretability, dynamics, and agent-based discovery. In this line of work, **PertDiffBench** (2025) provides a benchmark framework for systematically evaluating perturbation models under diverse biological settings, **pertXAI** (2026) explores how explainability methods can reveal biologically meaningful patterns from model behavior, and **MEGA-ODE** (2026) investigates dynamic modeling of perturbed cells to better characterize how cellular states evolve under interventions. Building on these directions, I am also exploring **pertAgent**, an ongoing effort to study how AI agents can support perturbation analysis, biological reasoning, and hypothesis generation.

## Computational Methods for Gene Regulation

<img src="/researchExperience/GeneRegulation_Summary.png" alt="Gene regulation summary" style="max-width: 100%; height: auto;">

Another line of my research concerns computational methods for gene regulation, spanning transcriptional regulation, gene regulatory network inference, and post-translational regulation. In this area, **ChiMER** (2026) was developed for identifying chimeric enhancer RNAs from short RNA-seq reads and studying enhancer-mediated transcriptional regulation, **TPDAinfer** (2018) provides a Bayesian network-based approach for causal gene regulatory network construction from omics data, and **VPTMdb** (2021) supports the analysis of viral post-translational modifications and related regulatory mechanisms in virus-host systems.
